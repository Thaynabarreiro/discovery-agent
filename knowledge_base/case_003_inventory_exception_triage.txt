Case ID: CASE-003
Client Type: Specialty retail distributor
Industry: Retail and wholesale operations
Company Size: 65 employees

Initial Situation:
The client managed inventory exceptions across spreadsheets, supplier portals, and email. Stockouts were often discovered late, and operations managers spent hours reconciling conflicting information before escalating issues.

Core Problem:
The business needed a workflow that could detect inventory anomalies, summarize likely causes, and route exceptions to the right owner without requiring a full ERP replacement.

Current Tools:
- Excel
- Shopify
- Supplier CSV exports
- Email
- Slack

Solution Delivered:
A monitoring workflow ingested product and supplier files, identified stockout risk, matched exceptions against known patterns, and generated owner-specific action summaries.

Architecture Notes:
LangGraph was considered because exception handling can involve loops and conditional paths. For the first phase, CrewAI was chosen because the pilot scope was sequential and easier to validate: ingest, classify, summarize, escalate. A later phase could move to LangGraph if automated retries and conditional supplier workflows become central.

Business Outcome:
The operations team reduced weekly reconciliation from 9 hours to roughly 2 hours and caught priority stockout risks earlier.

Acceptance Criteria Used:
- Detect stockout risk for top 100 SKUs with at least 95% coverage.
- Generate exception summaries grouped by owner.
- Identify missing supplier files before analysis starts.
- Maintain an audit log of each recommendation.

Known Limitations:
- Supplier file formats changed without notice.
- SKU naming inconsistencies required a maintained mapping table.
- Automated supplier outreach was deferred until exception accuracy was validated.
