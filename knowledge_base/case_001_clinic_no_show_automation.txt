Case ID: CASE-001
Client Type: Multi-location dental clinic group
Industry: Healthcare services
Company Size: 42 employees

Initial Situation:
The client was losing revenue because patient no-shows were managed manually. Receptionists exported appointment lists from the scheduling system, copied patient details into spreadsheets, and sent WhatsApp reminders one by one. Missed follow-ups also made it hard to know which patients needed a second reminder.

Core Problem:
Manual reminder workflows were inconsistent, slow, and hard to audit. The team needed a safer process that could identify upcoming appointments, detect missing confirmations, and trigger the right reminder sequence.

Current Tools:
- Google Sheets
- WhatsApp Business
- Clinic scheduling system
- Gmail

Solution Delivered:
A lightweight automation pipeline pulled appointment data every morning, normalized patient records, classified confirmation status, and generated reminder tasks. The system included human approval before sending messages and a daily operations report.

Architecture Notes:
CrewAI was appropriate because the process had clear sequential roles: intake, data validation, message drafting, and operations summary. No complex loop or graph state was required. ChromaDB was used only for internal examples of approved reminder copy and escalation patterns.

Business Outcome:
The clinic reduced manual reminder work from roughly 3 hours per day to under 35 minutes. The operations lead gained a daily view of pending confirmations and exceptions.

Acceptance Criteria Used:
- Import appointment list with at least 98% row-level parsing accuracy.
- Flag duplicate or incomplete patient records before message generation.
- Generate reminder drafts for all appointments within the next 72 hours.
- Produce a daily exception report by 9:00 AM local time.

Known Limitations:
- Patient consent rules had to be confirmed before sending automated messages.
- Scheduling system exports were inconsistent across locations.
- WhatsApp delivery failures required a manual fallback process.
